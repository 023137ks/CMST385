Joshua Bell

Joshua Bell is a GRAMMY® award-winning violinist. He began playing at age four and made his Carnegie Hall debut at age 17. His first album was released at age 18 and since then he has continued recording and has received many accolades and awards.

Bell succeeded founder Sir Neville Mariner as Music Director for the Academy of St. Martin in the Field, recording several albums, including Bruch: Scottish Fantasy which was nomimated for a GRAMMY® award. Bell won a GRAMMY® award for his recording of Nicholas Maw's Violin Concerto.

Bell has appeared on numerous film soundtracks including The Red Violin, Ladies in Lavender, and Defiance. He appeared as himself in the Amazon series Mozart in the Jungle and has performed multiple times on The Tonight Show.

In 2007, Bell played in the Washington, D.C. subway "incognito" and the subsequent article written about the event won a Pulitzer prize for The Washington Post.

Bell plays the 1713 Huberman Stradivarius violin.
